Chakrasana

चक्रासन  ·  Wheel Pose

VideoObject schema active  ·  Google Rich Result eligible

About the pose

Chakrasana, or Wheel Pose, is a deep backbend that opens the chest, shoulders, and hip flexors while strengthening the arms, wrists, and legs. It is one of the most energising postures in the Hatha yoga tradition, often used to counter the effects of prolonged sitting and forward-bending work.

Benefits

  • Stretches the spine and increases vertebral flexibility
  • Opens the chest and improves respiratory capacity
  • Strengthens wrists, arms, and shoulder girdle
  • Stimulates the thyroid and pituitary glands
  • Energises the nervous system and reduces fatigue

Contraindications

  • Carpal tunnel syndrome or wrist injury
  • Back injury or recent spinal surgery
  • High or low blood pressure
  • Headache or migraine
